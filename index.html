    Framework philosophy

    Introduction to the Product Lifecycle Framework

    The Product Lifecycle Framework is Evidi's common structure for managing, developing, improving, and scaling products and services across their entire lifecycle. It helps product teams, leaders, architects, consultants, and business stakeholders understand where a product stands, which capabilities already exist, and what is needed to reach a higher maturity level.

    The framework creates a shared language for product management across the organization and connects strategy, customer understanding, product development, launch activities, operational governance, and long-term lifecycle management into one coherent model.

    Understanding the structure

    The center of the illustration represents the Product Management Framework itself. Around it are the capability domains that enable successful product management. The framework is divided into four perspectives: Product Planning, Product Development, Product Introduction, and Product Life Cycle Management.

    Supporting leadership and operational capabilities sit on the left side of the model and ensure the organization has the governance, collaboration models, tools, and competencies needed for product excellence over time.

    Intended use

    Teams can use the framework to assess product maturity, identify capability gaps, define improvement initiatives, align around product goals, support governance and decision-making, improve cross-departmental collaboration, and create more predictable product outcomes.

    Leaders can use it to align investments and priorities, while product managers can use it to structure work and clarify responsibilities across the lifecycle.

    Innovation taxonomy and classification

    Not every innovation we create should be managed as a product. In practice, innovation appears in different forms with different ownership models, investment profiles, and lifecycle expectations. To create clarity across teams, this portal introduces a working taxonomy that helps classify initiatives before we assign product-level commitments. Product is one possible destination, not the default destination.
